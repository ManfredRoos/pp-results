How do we Predict Subcellular Localisation

Subcellular localization is predicted by LocTree2, a system of hierarchically organized Support Vector Machines, implemented to imitate protein sorting mechanisms. Predictions are based on evolutionary information derived from sequence homologs. LocTree2 predicts transmembrane and water-soluble globular proteins in the the so far largest number of compartments:
  • localization classes for Eukaryota: chloroplast, chloroplast membrane, cytosol, ER, Golgi, ER membrane, Golgi membrane, extra-cellular, mitochondria, mitochondria membrane, nucleus, nucleus membrane, peroxisome, peroxisome membrane, plasma membrane, plastid, vacuole and vacuole membrane
  • classes for Bacteria: cytosol, extra-cellular, fimbrium, outer membrane, periplasmic space and plasma membrane
  • classes for Archaea: cytosol, extra-cellular and plasma membrane
